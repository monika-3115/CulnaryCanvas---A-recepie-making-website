Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie


Smooth and rich homemade chocolate pudding with a hint of espresso nestles between an Oreo cookie crust and a mocha whipped cream topping in this delectable mocha chocolate pudding pie. This pie is a perfect make-ahead dessert because the pudding needs to set before garnishing and serving.




Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie details:


Texture:

This Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie boasts a luscious and creamy texture, with a velvety smooth pudding filling that melts in your mouth. The chocolate cookie crust adds a delightful crunch to each bite, creating a perfect contrast of textures.


Flavor:

This pie is a chocolate lover's dream, with rich cocoa flavor complemented by the boldness of espresso or coffee. The bittersweet chocolate adds depth and complexity, while the touch of vanilla extract enhances the overall aroma.


Ease:

Despite its decadent appearance, this pie is surprisingly easy to make. The crust comes together quickly with just two ingredients and a simple press-and-bake method. The pudding filling is made on the stovetop with basic pantry staples, and it requires only a few minutes of cooking time.


Time:

The total time required to make this Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie is approximately 30 minutes for preparation and assembly, plus an additional 4 hours of chilling time in the refrigerator to set the filling. Overall, it's a relatively quick dessert to whip up, making it perfect for both casual weeknight treats and special occasions when you need a show-stopping dessert without spending hours in the kitchen.



Ingredients


Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie:

For the crust:
1 1/2 cups chocolate cookie crumbs
6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted





Filling Ingredients:


1/3 cup granulated sugar
3 tablespoons cornstarch
2 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
1 1/2 cups whole milk
1/2 cup heavy cream
2 tablespoons instant espresso powder or instant coffee granules
4 ounces bittersweet chocolate, finely chopped
1 teaspoon vanilla extract


For Topping:


1 cup heavy cream
2 tablespoons powdered sugar
Chocolate shavings or cocoa powder for garnish (optional)















1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a bowl, mix together the chocolate cookie crumbs and melted butter until well combined.

2.Press the mixture evenly into the bottom and up the sides of a 9-inch pie dish. Bake the crust for 8-10 minutes, then remove from the oven and let it cool completely.

3. In a medium saucepan, whisk together the granulated sugar, cornstarch, and cocoa powder. Gradually whisk in the milk and heavy cream until smooth. Place the saucepan over medium heat and cook, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil, about 5-7 minutes.

4.Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the instant espresso powder (or coffee granules) and chopped bittersweet chocolate until the chocolate is completely melted and the mixture is smooth. Stir in the vanilla extract.

5. Pour the mocha chocolate pudding filling into the cooled crust and smooth the top with a spatula. Place a piece of plastic wrap directly on the surface of the pudding to prevent a skin from forming. Chill the pie in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours, or until set.

6. Before serving, whip the heavy cream and powdered sugar together until soft peaks form. Spread the whipped cream over the chilled pie. Garnish with chocolate shavings or a dusting of cocoa powder, if desired.

7.Slice the pie and serve chilled. Enjoy the creamy, chocolaty goodness of your Mocha Chocolate Pudding Pie!
