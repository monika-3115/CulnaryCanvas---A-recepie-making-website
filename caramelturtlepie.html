Caramel Turtle Brownie Pie


Texture:

The texture of this dessert would likely be rich, indulgent, and decadent. The brownie layer would be dense and fudgy, while the caramel layer would add a gooey and sticky texture. Additionally, any nuts (typically pecans in a turtle dessert) would provide a crunchy contrast to the softness of the brownie and caramel layers.


Flavor:

The flavor profile of a Caramel Turtle Brownie Pie would be a symphony of sweetness and richness. The brownie layer would offer a deep chocolate flavor with hints of cocoa, while the caramel layer would bring a sweet, buttery, and slightly salty taste. The addition of pecans would contribute a nutty flavor and a pleasant crunch, reminiscent of classic turtle candies.


Ease:

While this dessert sounds fancy, it can be quite simple to prepare, especially if you use pre-made components like store-bought brownie mix or caramel sauce. You would essentially need to prepare or buy a brownie batter, bake it in a pie dish, pour over a layer of caramel sauce (either homemade or store-bought), sprinkle with chopped pecans, and bake until set. It's a straightforward process that doesn't require advanced baking skills.


Time:

The time required to make a Caramel Turtle Brownie Pie would depend on whether you're making everything from scratch or utilizing pre-made ingredients. If you're making the brownie batter from scratch, it might take a bit longer. However, if you're using a store-bought brownie mix and caramel sauce, the preparation time would be shorter. In total, you're looking at around 1 to 1.5 hours, including prep time and baking time



Ingredients


Caramel Turtle Brownie Pie


For the Brownie Layer:


1/2 cup (115g) unsalted butter
1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
2 large eggs
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/3 cup (40g) cocoa powder
1/2 cup (65g) all-purpose flour
1/4 teaspoon salt

For Caramel Layer:


1 cup (220g) packed brown sugar
1/2 cup (120ml) heavy cream
4 tablespoons (56g) unsalted butter
1/2 teaspoon salt
1 teaspoon vanilla extract









1.Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch pie dish. In a microwave-safe bowl, melt the butter in the microwave. Allow it to cool slightly.

2.In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together the sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract. Gradually add the melted butter to the sugar mixture, whisking continuously.

3.Sift in the cocoa powder, flour, and salt. Mix until well combined. Pour the brownie batter into the prepared pie dish and spread it out evenly.

4.In a saucepan over medium heat, combine the brown sugar, heavy cream, butter, and salt. Stir continuously until the mixture comes to a simmer.

5.Let it simmer for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it thickens slightly. Remove the caramel from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract.

6.Pour the caramel evenly over the brownie batter in the pie dish. Sprinkle the chopped pecans over the caramel layer.

7.Place the pie dish in the preheated oven and bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the edges are set and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out with a few moist crumbs. Remove from the oven and let it cool slightly before serving.
