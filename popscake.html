Chocolate Cake Pops


Chocolate cake + chocolate frosting + cute heart sprinkles = the most adorable treat! Today we’re making chocolate cake pops because many of you request them, especially after I shared my vanilla cake pops. Your wish is my command. What I love most, however, is that you have creative control. You can decorate these cake pops in your own unique way, which makes this recipe 100x more fun.




Chocolate Cake Pops Details:


Texture:

Chocolate cake pops typically have a moist and dense texture, similar to that of cake truffles. The cake is crumbled and mixed with frosting to create a dough-like consistency, which is then rolled into balls. Once coated in chocolate and chilled, the cake pops have a firm exterior with a soft and slightly gooey interior, providing a satisfying contrast in textures.


Flavor:

The flavor of chocolate cake pops is rich and decadent, with a deep chocolate taste from both the cake and the coating. Depending on the recipe, you may use cocoa powder or melted chocolate in the cake batter, adding layers of chocolatey goodness. Additionally, the frosting used to bind the cake crumbs together can enhance the sweetness and creaminess of the overall flavor profile.


Time:

Making chocolate cake pops from start to finish can take approximately 3-4 hours, including cooling and chilling times. It's a fun and rewarding baking project that yields delicious bite-sized treats perfect for any occasion!


Ingredients


Chocolate Cake Pops Cake:


1 cup (125g) all-purpose flour (spooned & leveled)
1 cup (200g) granulated sugar
6 Tablespoons (32g) unsweetened natural cocoa powder (1/3 cup + 1 Tbsp)
1/2 teaspoon baking soda
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup (120mll) canola, vegetable, or melted coconut oil
2 large eggs, at room temperature
1 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
Chocolate Frosting:
6 Tablespoons (85g) unsalted butter, softened to room temperature
3/4 cup (90g) confectioners’ sugar
1/2 cup (41g) unsweetened natural or dutch-process cocoa powder
2–3 teaspoons heavy cream or milk
1/2 teaspoon pure vanilla extract
32 ounces candy melts or coating (or pure chocolate)* sprinkles




1. Baking the Cake: This typically takes around 25-35 minutes, depending on the recipe and the size of the cake pan.

2. Cooling the Cake: Allow the baked cake to cool completely before proceeding, which can take about 1-2 hours.

3. Making the Cake Balls: Crumbling the cooled cake and mixing it with frosting usually takes around 15-20 minutes.

4. Rolling and Shaping: Forming the cake mixture into balls can take another 15-20 minutes, depending on the number of cake pops you're making and your desired size.

5. Chilling Time: Once shaped, the cake pops need to chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour to firm up.

6. Coating and Decorating: Dipping the chilled cake pops in melted chocolate and decorating them with sprinkles or other toppings can take around 30-45 minutes, depending on your decorating skills and preferences.

7. Final Chilling: After decorating, allow the cake pops to chill for another 15-30 minutes to set the chocolate coating.
